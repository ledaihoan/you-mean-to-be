We started with a simple question: **what if learning felt like exploring a universe?**

Most education platforms are either dry and transactional — click, watch, quiz, repeat — or so gamified that the game becomes the point. YouMeanToBe sits between Khan Academy and a science museum. We believe the best learning happens when curiosity pulls you forward, not when a progress bar pushes you.

## The Three Pillars

This platform has three interlocking parts that reinforce each other:

**1. Immersive Simulations**
When you can *see* why spiral arms in galaxies don't wind up, when you can manipulate the parameters and watch the math come alive — that changes how knowledge settles in your brain. Simulations aren't decoration. They're the pedagogy.

**2. Writing as Thinking**
Every simulation page has a blog post alongside it. Not just "here's how it works" but "here's what we don't know yet, here's the frontier." We want to model what intellectual curiosity looks like in practice.

**3. Healthy Growth**
Mind and body aren't separate. Nutrition science, sleep research, movement biology — these deserve their own interactive spaces too. We'll get there in Phase 2.

## What We're Not

We won't build a social network, at least not at first. We won't have leaderboards or streaks or engagement metrics. We believe in intrinsic motivation — the feeling you get when something *clicks*.

## The Technical Choices

Built on Next.js 14 with React Three Fiber for the 3D work. Auth via better-auth (runs inside the app, no vendor lock-in). Postgres + Redis, self-hosted on a home server exposed through Cloudflare Tunnel. All of this matters because we want full ownership.

The simulations use custom GLSL shaders for the cinematic stuff — the galaxy scene you'll find on this platform isn't a point cloud asset. It's a procedural generation. That's the level of craft we want to bring to education.
